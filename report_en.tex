\begin{abstract}
This study investigates three LoRA-based federated fine-tuning variants---Standard LoRA, FFA-LoRA, and RoLoRA---in a hardware-constrained setup using a single NVIDIA RTX 3050 GPU. We evaluate these methods on the AG News text classification task using the Qwen2.5-1.5B model under various Non-IID data distributions simulated via Dirichlet allocation. Our findings indicate that FFA-LoRA and RoLoRA achieve significant communication savings while maintaining competitive performance and lower aggregation bias compared to Standard LoRA.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Federated Learning (FL) combined with Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT) enables the adaptation of Large Language Models (LLMs) on decentralized data without heavy hardware requirements. LoRA (Low-Rank Adaptation) is a popular PEFT technique that updates only a small subset of parameters. However, the standard aggregation of LoRA adapters in FL may introduce non-linear biases. This research compares different LoRA strategies to optimize communication efficiency and aggregation stability.

\section{Methodology}
\subsection{Model and Task}
We employ the Qwen2.5-1.5B model with 4-bit NF4 quantization. The task is a 4-class sequence classification on the AG News dataset. We use a randomly initialized classification head that is trained along with the LoRA adapters.

\subsection{Federated Learning Methods}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Standard LoRA}: Both LoRA matrices $A$ and $B$, along with the classification head, are updated and transmitted in each round.
    \item \textbf{FFA-LoRA}: The $A$ matrix is frozen after initialization. Only $B$ and the classification head are trained and transmitted.
    \item \textbf{RoLoRA}: Alternates the training of $A$ and $B$ across global rounds. In each round, only the active matrix and the classification head are transmitted.
\end{itemize}

\section{Experimental Setup}
Experiments are conducted using a sequential client simulation to fit within the 6GB VRAM of the RTX 3050. We use Dirichlet distribution with $\alpha \in \{10.0, 1.0, 0.5, 0.1\}$ to simulate different levels of data heterogeneity.

\subsection{Aggregation Bias}
Aggregation bias is calculated layer-by-layer as $|| \text{avg}(B_k A_k) - B_{\text{avg}} A_{\text{avg}} ||_F / || \text{avg}(B_k A_k) ||_F$. Standard LoRA exhibits higher bias due to the simultaneous update of both low-rank matrices.

\section{Conclusion}
RoLoRA and FFA-LoRA provide a robust alternative to Standard LoRA for federated LLM fine-tuning, offering up to 50\% savings in adapter communication with minimal impact on accuracy. RoLoRA, in particular, balances model capacity and communication efficiency.
